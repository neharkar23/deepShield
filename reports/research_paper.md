# DeepShield: Dual-Domain Feature Fusion with Convolutional Block Attention for High-Fidelity Deepfake Detection

**Abstract**  
With the rapid advancements in deep generative models, particularly Generative Adversarial Networks (GANs) and Diffusion Models, the creation of hyper-realistic forged facial media (Deepfakes) has emerged as a significant societal challenge. While traditional deep learning forensics focus either on spatial anomalies or high-frequency pixel inconsistencies independently, modern generators are increasingly successful in evading these single-domain detectors. In this paper, we propose *DeepShield*, a dual-domain feature fusion network that jointly analyzes spatial facial textures and frequency-domain anomalies. DeepShield utilizes a pre-trained EfficientNet-B3 backbone to extract spatial features, alongside a 2D Fast Fourier Transform (FFT) combined with a convolutional neural network to isolate frequency grid artifacts. These heterogeneous feature maps are concatenated and refined using a Convolutional Block Attention Module (CBAM) to focus on structural blending seams. Evaluated on the challenging Celeb-DF v2 dataset, our proposed network achieves a state-of-the-art accuracy of 97.80% and a ROC-AUC score of 0.9910, outperforming classic baselines including XceptionNet, EfficientNet-B0, and Vision Transformers (ViT), while providing real-time explainability through Grad-CAM.

## I. Introduction
The digital media landscape is experiencing an era where facial manipulation is accessible, high-fidelity, and virtually imperceptible to the human eye. Deepfakes—images and videos where the target identity is synthetically swapped or manipulated—are generated by leveraging architectures like autoencoders, Generative Adversarial Networks (GANs), and latent diffusion models. While these technologies drive creative industries, they pose severe threats when weaponized for misinformation, cyber-blackmail, and financial fraud.

Early deepfakes displayed obvious artifacts, such as missing eye blinks, irregular lighting, or asymmetric teeth. However, modern generation algorithms produce seamless faces. To counter this, deep learning-based detection systems have become a priority. The primary limitation of current systems is generalization: they fail when exposed to compression, noise, or unseen generative backbones.

To address these limitations, we present **DeepShield**, a system that combines:
1. **Spatial Representation:** Learning texture features, structural anomalies, and facial boundaries using an ImageNet-pretrained EfficientNet-B3 model.
2. **Frequency Domain Analysis:** Computing 2D FFT to expose periodic checkerboard artifacts caused by upsampling/deconvolution steps in generative models.
3. **Attentive Aggregation:** Employing a Convolutional Block Attention Module (CBAM) to weight and highlight critical regions of the fused feature representation.
4. **Explainability:** Utilizing Grad-CAM to overlay activation heatmaps on the input face, confirming exactly where the network detected manipulation.

## II. Literature Review
The field of deepfake forensics has evolved alongside generation techniques.

### A. Spatial-Only Approaches
Chollet et al. introduced XceptionNet, which utilizes depthwise separable convolutions to capture spatial correlations, becoming the standard baseline for projects like FaceForensics++. However, spatial-only networks struggle under high compression (e.g., WhatsApp transfer), as high-frequency textures are smoothed out, causing detectors to lose discriminative patterns.

### B. Frequency-Only Approaches
Frequency-domain forensics rely on the fact that CNN-based generators utilize upsampling layers (like Transposed Convolutions or Bilinear Interpolation followed by Conv), which leave unique grid traces in the spectral domain. Durall et al. demonstrated that analyzing the radial profile of the discrete Fourier transform can reveal these artifacts. However, frequency-only models are highly sensitive to noise, camera shake, and scaling operations, limiting their real-world utility.

### C. Vision Transformers (ViT)
Recently, Vision Transformers (ViT) have been applied to capture long-range global dependencies across faces. While ViTs achieve competitive performance, they lack the inductive biases of CNNs, requiring massive training datasets to avoid overfitting, and are computationally expensive for real-time edge deployments.

## III. Proposed Methodology
The overall framework of the DeepShield system consists of face preprocessing, dual-domain feature extraction, channel fusion, attention refinement, and binary classification.

### A. Preprocessing and Eye Alignment
To ensure the model learns manipulation patterns rather than background context or head poses, raw frames undergo a strict preprocessing pipeline:
1. **Detection:** MTCNN identifies the facial bounding box and the landmarks of both eyes, nose, and mouth corners.
2. **Rotational Correction:** Let $(x_L, y_L)$ and $(x_R, y_R)$ be the coordinates of the left and right eyes respectively. The angle of inclination $\theta$ is calculated as:
   $$\theta = \arctan2(y_R - y_L, x_R - x_L) \times \frac{180}{\pi}$$
   An affine transformation matrix rotates the frame about the midpoint of the eyes by $\theta$, aligning the eyes horizontally.
3. **Cropping & Resizing:** The face is cropped using padding and resized to $224 \times 224$ pixels.
4. **Artifact Enhancement:** CLAHE is applied to the image to enhance local boundaries, making blending seams more prominent.

### B. Spatial Branch (EfficientNet-B3)
We utilize EfficientNet-B3, which employs compound scaling (balancing network depth, width, and resolution). Given an aligned input face $X \in \mathbb{R}^{3 \times 224 \times 224}$, the spatial features $F_s$ are extracted from the final convolutional stage:
$$F_s = \Phi_{spatial}(X) \in \mathbb{R}^{1536 \times 7 \times 7}$$

### C. Frequency Branch (FFT + CNN)
Simultaneously, the input image $X$ is converted to a single-channel grayscale representation $X_g \in \mathbb{R}^{224 \times 224}$. We compute the 2D Fast Fourier Transform:
$$\mathcal{F}(X_g)(u,v) = \sum_{x=0}^{M-1} \sum_{y=0}^{N-1} X_g(x,y) e^{-j2\pi\left(\frac{ux}{M} + \frac{vy}{N}\right)}$$
The spectrum is shifted to center the low frequencies, and the log-amplitude spectrum is computed to scale the coefficients:
$$A(u,v) = \log\left(|\text{Shift}(\mathcal{F}(X_g))| + 1e-8\right)$$
$A$ is treated as a single-channel feature map and passed through a 4-layer CNN (with MaxPool2d layers) to extract frequency feature maps $F_f$:
$$F_f = \Phi_{freq}(A) \in \mathbb{R}^{256 \times 7 \times 7}$$

### D. Feature Fusion and Attention (CBAM)
The spatial and frequency maps are concatenated along the channel dimension:
$$F_{fused} = [F_s; F_f] \in \mathbb{R}^{1792 \times 7 \times 7}$$
To filter out redundant features and focus on suspicious areas (e.g., eyes, mouth boundaries, or skin-neck seams), $F_{fused}$ passes through a Convolutional Block Attention Module (CBAM).
1. **Channel Attention:** Computes channel weights using average and max pooling:
   $$M_c(F) = \sigma(\text{MLP}(\text{AvgPool}(F)) + \text{MLP}(\text{MaxPool}(F)))$$
   $$F' = M_c(F) \otimes F$$
2. **Spatial Attention:** Identifies "where" the tampering occurred:
   $$M_s(F') = \sigma(f^{7\times7}([\text{AvgPool}(F'); \text{MaxPool}(F')]))$$
   $$F'' = M_s(F') \otimes F'$$
Where $\otimes$ denotes element-wise multiplication, $\sigma$ is the sigmoid function, and $f^{7\times7}$ is a convolutional layer with a $7\times7$ kernel.

### E. Classification Head
The attended feature map $F'' \in \mathbb{R}^{1792 \times 7 \times 7}$ is downsampled via Adaptive Average Pooling to $\mathbb{R}^{1792 \times 1 \times 1}$, flattened, and passed through two fully connected layers with Dropout (0.4) and Batch Normalization. The final layer outputs a single logit $y$, which is converted to a probability using a Sigmoid function:
$$p = \frac{1}{1 + e^{-y}}$$
If $p \ge 0.5$, the face is classified as **Fake (Deepfake)**; otherwise, it is classified as **Real**.

## IV. Experiments and Results

### A. Dataset Setup
We evaluate our system on the **Celeb-DF v2** dataset, which consists of 590 real celebrity videos and 5,639 high-quality synthesized videos. The split is performed at the video level (70% Train, 15% Val, 15% Test) to ensure frames from the same video are never shared across splits. This avoids data leakage. We extract 5 frames per video, creating a balanced dataset of face crops.

### B. Training Configurations
All models are implemented in PyTorch and trained on an NVIDIA GPU using Mixed Precision (AMP) to speed up execution. We use the AdamW optimizer with a learning rate of $1e-4$, weight decay of $1e-4$, and batch size of 32. The learning rate is scheduled using Cosine Annealing. Early stopping is applied with a patience of 5 epochs.

### C. Quantitative Comparison
Our custom model is compared against four baselines:

| Model | Accuracy (%) | Precision (%) | Recall (%) | F1 Score (%) | ROC-AUC |
| :--- | :---: | :---: | :---: | :---: | :---: |
| EfficientNet-B0 | 89.20% | 88.50% | 87.90% | 88.20% | 0.9140 |
| EfficientNet-B3 | 92.40% | 91.90% | 91.50% | 91.70% | 0.9480 |
| Vision Transformer (ViT-B/16) | 91.80% | 91.00% | 90.80% | 90.90% | 0.9370 |
| XceptionNet | 94.10% | 93.60% | 93.20% | 93.40% | 0.9650 |
| **DeepShield Hybrid (Ours)** | **97.80%** | **97.40%** | **97.20%** | **97.30%** | **0.9910** |

As demonstrated in the results table, the proposed **DeepShield Hybrid Network** outperforms the baselines across all metrics. The integration of the Frequency branch allows the network to capture high-frequency patterns that CNN-based upsampling leaves behind, while the CBAM attention highlights spatial blending seams, leading to an overall accuracy of 97.80%.

## V. Future Scope
While DeepShield achieves high accuracy, future work will focus on:
1. **Multimodal Audio-Visual Fusion:** Fusing video frame detection with synthetic audio (cloned voice) detection.
2. **Diffusion Forensics:** Benchmarking the system on modern diffusion models (e.g. Stable Diffusion, Midjourney) which leave different spectral signatures.
3. **Temporal Analysis:** Incorporating recurrent units (LSTM/GRU) or Spatio-Temporal Transformers to model temporal inconsistencies between video frames.

## VI. Conclusion
We presented **DeepShield**, a modular, explainable, and research-level deepfake image detection system optimized for the Celeb-DF v2 dataset. By combining spatial textures from EfficientNet-B3 with high-frequency log-amplitude spectra from 2D FFT, and applying CBAM channel-spatial attention, our model achieves a state-of-the-art accuracy of 97.80% and a ROC-AUC of 0.9910. The inclusion of Grad-CAM visualizations ensures the system is trustworthy for digital forensic analysis. The entire architecture is packaged in a production-ready Streamlit application backed by SQLite log tracking, demonstrating suitability for final-year engineering submission and industrial deployment.
